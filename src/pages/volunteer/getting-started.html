Volunteer Checklist

The purpose of this checklist is to provide you with a go-to resource for getting started with Prosper as a volunteer. We're always looking to make it easier for people to contribute to Prosper Projects and would appreciate any feedback you'd like to share.

Estimated time to complete all checklist items below: one hour.

▢ Request to volunteer

Submit the form on the Volunteer page. You may have already done this—if so, kudos!

▢ Receive an invitation

Once we receive your request to volunteer, we will review the details you've provided and send you an email with an invitation to join our community.

▢ Skim through the Playbook

Please give a quick skim through the Prosper Playbook to learn about what we're up to, our approach and the tools that we use.

▢ Read the RefugeeProjects Brief

Please read through the RefugeeProjects Brief to learn about our first and only active Prosper Project. If you don't read this carefully, it might be difficult to understand what is going on. Each checklist item below assumes you have read the brief.

▢ Join Prosper on Trello

Along with the invitation email, you should also receive an email to join the Prosper Community Team on Trello.

Trello is the tool that we use to manage projects. We have one Trello board for each project. To learn more about Trello and the tools we use, you might want to read through Prosper Playbook: Tools.

▢ Comment on a Trello Card in the Backlog

Open up the Prosper — RefugeeProjects Trello Board and comment on one of the cards in the 'Backlog'.

Commenting on a card lets us know you're up and running with Trello and you know how to comment—one of the key functionalities of working with teams on Trello.

If you're having trouble with this step, see Trello Help: Commening on cards

▢ Join Prosper on Slack

Along with inviting you to join Trello, we will also send you an invitation to join the Prosper Community Team on Slack.

We recommend downloading the Slack app on your computer and your phone. It's a great app and will make communicating much easier!

Slack is the tool that we use to communicate with other team members in real-time. Using Slack for the first time can be a bit intimidating: There are lots of conversations going on at the same time. Don't worry! This is just the nature of the tool.

▢ Introduce yourself on Slack

Once you've joined the Prosper Community Team on Slack, pleae introduce yourself in the #introductions channel. It's good for everyone to know:

  • Who you are
  • Why you joined
  • What skills / experience you have to offer

▢ Assign yourself to a task in Trello

Open up the Prosper — RefugeeProjects Trello Board and find a task that you'd like to take ownership of. Assign yourself to the task and move it into 'In Progress'.

You can comment on the card and ask if anyone else would like to work on this with you. Also, it is helpful to assign yourself a due date so that we know when you'd like to have this task completed by.

If you're having trouble adding yourself a task, take a look at Trello Help: Adding a member to a card. If you're having trouble adding a due date, see Trello Help: Adding due dates to cards.

▢ Complete the task, ask for review

Now that you've assigned yourself to a task and given yourself a due date, it would be great if you could complete the task. Once the task is complete, move it to 'In Review' by clicking and dragging the card.

If there is a specific member you'd like to review the task, please add them to the card and mention their name in a comment. If you need the task reviewed by a certain date, update the due date and let them know this in your comment.

When the task has been reviewed and your contribution has been accepted, the reviewer will move the task to 'Complete'. Congratulations! You've made your first contribution to a Prosper Project.

bonus

▢ Share what you've learned

If you learned anything while completing the task, it would be great to share what you learned. You can send a public message on Slack, or if you'd prefer to send something anonymously, please provide some feedback.

We believe that everyone has something to teach and something to learn. By encouraging our new team members to share what they learn in making their first contribution, we are fostering a learning culture that will help all of us grow.
