Overview

We are partnering with leading code schools to provide remote job training to help refugees land jobs as junior web developers (salaries starting around £25,000).

Each apprentice is paired with a life mentor whom they meet in person at least once a week. This allows apprentices to navigate through potentially challenging circumstances while developing their skills as web developers.

We are working with our code school partners to create a portfolio of hiring partners who offer guaranteed interviews at the end of the course with opportunities for full-time employability.

We provide specialized training for our mentors who work with refugees so that we can nuture well-being and self-confidence as well as employability. Being proficient in English is currently a pre-requisite, but we are constantly working to broaden the languages of our mentor group in order to support a more diverse community of apprentices.

First Cohort: March 2016

For our first cohort, we will be partnering with a world-class code school in London called Maker’s Academy who has offered places for Prosper Academy apprentices to attend their immersive online course called Ronin.

Ronin is remote, intensive and provides apprentices with everything they need to land their first job as a junior web developer.

The Maker’s Academy curriculum is based upon recognised industry standards and has successfully launched web development careers for hundreds of students from around the world.

Prosper Academy will support apprentices through applying to Maker’s Academy, preparing for the interview, completing the pre-coursework material, and successfully completing the Ronin curriculum.

If you’re interested in landing a job as a junior web developer, Prosper Academy is a place where you can enter as you are and leave with the confidence of knowing the beauty of your future that lies ahead.
