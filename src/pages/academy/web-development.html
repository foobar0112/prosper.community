Overview

The Web Development track at Prosper Academy is an apprenticeship for budding developers who want to grow their skills and maturity through contributing to refugee crisis projects.

custom curriculum

Rather than study a set curriculum like other design courses, our apprentices work with a career coach to develop a custom skill plan based upon their design experience, career goals and lifestyle needs.

With a custom skill plan in place, the apprentice gains experience and maturity through collaborating with other developers and designers on a Prosper Project. Every week, the apprentice gets to meet with their mentor and the project team. The program structure allows apprentices to learn how to work in an Agile environment and how to design web-based products with Lean methodologies.

this track is designed for:

  • Developers who are passionate about learning how to use open source web technologies to solve problems of the refugee crisis

  • Citizens and refugees who have experience developing websites / applications but need more skills and experience to land a dream job

  • Self-guided learners who know the value of collaborating with a mentor and other developers

  • Developers who can perservere through thorny problems to craft elegant code-based solutions

this track is not designed for:

  • Complete beginners with no experience developing websites.

  • People who need hand-holding to complete tasks on real projects.

  • People who discriminate against asylum seekers and migrants.

Skill Plans

While we do create custom skill plans for each apprentice, we have found it helpful to provide some sample skill plans for apprentices to pick and choose the types of skills they'd like to learn. Feel free to use these plans as a reference for the type of plan you'll customize with your career coach when you start the program.

front-end

I want to specialize in bringing user-centered designs to life using semantic front-end code. I love the craft of building websites and there are specific technologies and skills I'd like to learn including:

  • Running in sprints on an agile team
  • Creating custom Gulp tasks and development workflows
  • Writing semantic, assessible HTML5 markup
  • Writing scalable modular CSS and SCSS
  • Crafting mobile-first responsive websites
  • Developing patterns rather than pages
  • Collaborating with designers to execute designs

back-end

I want to dive deep into the world of web architecture, data and programming. I love the elegant simplicity of writing clean, terse code. I do have some experience working in Ruby and PHP but I've decided I want to learn the following skills and technologies to advance my career:

  • Architecting Ruby on Rails applications
  • Strengthening TDD practices
  • Writing modular code
  • Understanding NoSql databases
  • Pair programming
  • Working with Social API's like Twitter and Facebook
